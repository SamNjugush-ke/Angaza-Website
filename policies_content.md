# Angaza Future International - Organizational Policies

## 1. Financial Management Policy

**Purpose**: Ensure transparent, accountable, and efficient management of organizational finances in compliance with Kenyan legal requirements and international development standards.

**Key Principles**:
- All financial transactions must be documented and authorized according to delegation of authority
- Segregation of duties between authorization, recording, and reconciliation
- Monthly financial reporting and quarterly reviews with Board oversight
- Annual independent external audit by qualified auditors registered with ICPAK
- Compliance with NGO Coordination Board requirements and PBO Act provisions

**Scope**: Covers budgeting, procurement, cash management, donor fund management, and financial reporting.

**Implementation**:
- Finance Committee reviews monthly statements and quarterly reports
- Annual budgets developed through participatory process involving all departments
- Donor funds managed in separate accounts with dedicated tracking
- Currency management policies for foreign exchange transactions
- Bank reconciliation performed monthly by independent staff

**Responsibilities**:
- Finance Manager: Daily financial operations and compliance
- Finance Committee: Oversight and policy review
- Board: Approval of annual budgets and audited accounts
- External Auditors: Annual independent verification

## 2. Procurement Policy

**Purpose**: Establish transparent, competitive, and value-for-money procurement processes that comply with Kenyan public procurement regulations and development partner requirements.

**Key Principles**:
- Competitive bidding for all procurements above KES 100,000
- Three quotations minimum for purchases between KES 50,000-100,000
- Direct procurement permitted for amounts below KES 50,000 with documentation
- Preference for local suppliers and MSMEs where quality and cost are competitive
- Environmental and social considerations in vendor selection

**Procurement Categories**:
- **Goods**: Office supplies, equipment, training materials
- **Services**: Consultancies, training delivery, technical support
- **Works**: Office renovation, infrastructure development

**Compliance**:
- Adherence to donor procurement guidelines (World Bank, DFID, EU standards where applicable)
- Documentation of all procurement decisions with supporting evidence
- Conflict of interest declarations from procurement committee members
- Regular supplier performance reviews

**Implementation**:
- Procurement Committee reviews all procurements above KES 100,000
- Vendor management system maintained with performance ratings
- Annual procurement plan aligned with organizational budget
- Whistleblower mechanism for procurement irregularities

## 3. Human Resource Policy

**Compliance**:

**Special Provisions**:

## 4. Anti-Fraud and Corruption Policy

**Purpose**: Prevent, detect, and respond to fraud and corruption to protect organizational assets and maintain stakeholder trust.

**Definitions**:
- **Fraud**: Intentional deception for personal or organizational gain
- **Corruption**: Abuse of position for personal benefit
- **Bribery**: Offering or receiving gifts/favors to influence decisions
- **Embezzlement**: Theft of organizational funds or assets

**Prevention Measures**:
- Clear segregation of duties in financial processes
- Regular internal audits and spot checks
- Competitive procurement and transparent vendor management
- Staff code of conduct with mandatory training
- Whistleblower protection and anonymous reporting mechanisms
- Regular fraud risk assessments

**Detection**:
- Internal audit function with quarterly reviews
- External audit by independent auditors
- Beneficiary feedback mechanisms
- Anonymous hotline for reporting concerns
- Surprise cash counts and asset verifications

**Response**:
- Investigation procedures with due process
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Recovery of misappropriated funds
- Reporting to relevant authorities (Police, NGO Board)
- Organizational learning and system improvements

## 8. Data Protection & Privacy Policy

**Purpose**: Protect personal data and privacy of beneficiaries, staff, and stakeholders in compliance with Kenya's Data Protection Act, 2019.

**Principles**:
- Lawfulness, fairness, and transparency in data collection
- Purpose limitation - data used only for stated purposes
- Data minimization - collect only necessary data
- Accuracy and keeping data up to date
- Storage limitation - retain data only as long as necessary
- Integrity and confidentiality with appropriate security

**Data Collection**:
- Informed consent from data subjects
- Clear privacy notices explaining data use
- Legitimate basis for processing (consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interests)
- Opt-out mechanisms where applicable
- Parental consent for data on children

**Data Security**:
- Encrypted storage of sensitive data
- Access controls limiting who can view data
- Regular security audits and updates
- Incident response procedures
- Staff training on data protection
- Secure data destruction procedures

**Rights**:
- Right to access personal data held
- Right to rectification of inaccurate data
- Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
- Right to restrict processing
- Right to data portability
- Right to object to processing

**Data Breach Response**:
- Immediate notification to affected individuals
- Reporting to Data Protection Commissioner where required
- Investigation and remedial action
- Documentation and learning

## 9. Conflict of Interest Policy

**Purpose**: Identify, disclose, and manage conflicts of interest to maintain organizational integrity and public trust.

**Definition**: A conflict of interest exists when personal interests (financial or non-financial) could influence or appear to influence professional judgment.

**Scope**: Applies to Board members, staff, volunteers, and contractors.

**Disclosure Requirements**:
- Annual declaration of interests (financial holdings, family relationships, external positions)
- Disclosure of specific conflicts before decisions are made
- Regular updates when circumstances change
- Confidential handling of disclosure information

**Management Procedures**:
- Recusal from decisions where conflict exists
- Abstention from voting on related matters
- Documentation of conflicts and management actions
- Review by Board or management as appropriate
- No retaliation for good faith disclosures

**Examples of Conflicts**:
- Financial interest in vendors or service providers
- Family relationships with staff or Board members
- External employment or directorships
- Personal relationships affecting professional judgment
- Competing organizational loyalties

**Enforcement**:
- Breach of policy may result in disciplinary action
- Regular audit of compliance
- Training for all staff on conflict of interest
- Whistleblower protection for reporting concerns

## 10. Whistleblower Protection Policy

**Purpose**: Encourage reporting of suspected wrongdoing while protecting whistleblowers from retaliation.

**Protected Disclosures**:
- Suspected fraud, corruption, or financial irregularities
- Violations of law or organizational policy
- Unsafe or unethical practices
- Misuse of organizational resources
- Safeguarding concerns
- Any conduct contrary to organizational values

**Reporting Channels**:
- Direct supervisor or manager
- Finance Manager or Finance Committee Chair
- Executive Director
- Board Chair
- Anonymous hotline (third-party managed)
- Email: whistleblower@angazafuture.org
- In-person meeting with designated officer

**Protections**:
- Confidentiality of whistleblower identity (unless disclosure required by law)
- No retaliation, discrimination, or adverse action
- Protection from dismissal or demotion
- Legal remedies available if retaliation occurs
- Support and counseling services

**Investigation Process**:
- Prompt acknowledgment of report
- Impartial investigation by designated officer
- Opportunity for accused to respond
- Confidential handling of information
- Findings and recommendations
- Corrective action as appropriate
- Feedback to whistleblower (within confidentiality constraints)

**False Reports**:
- Good faith reports protected even if not substantiated
- Deliberate false reports may result in disciplinary action
- Distinction between unsubstantiated and false reports

## 11. Risk Management Policy

**Purpose**: Identify, assess, and manage organizational risks to ensure sustainable operations and achievement of strategic objectives.

**Risk Categories**:
- **Financial**: Budget shortfalls, currency fluctuations, donor funding delays
- **Operational**: Staff turnover, system failures, supply chain disruptions
- **Compliance**: Regulatory changes, audit findings, legal challenges
- **Reputational**: Safeguarding incidents, fraud, negative media
- **Strategic**: Market changes, competition, partnership failures
- **External**: Political instability, security threats, natural disasters

**Risk Management Process**:
- **Identification**: Regular risk assessment workshops and reviews
- **Assessment**: Likelihood and impact rating (High/Medium/Low)
- **Response**: Mitigation strategies, acceptance, transfer, or avoidance
- **Monitoring**: Quarterly review of key risks
- **Reporting**: Annual risk register to Board

**Risk Mitigation Examples**:
- Financial: Diversified donor base, cash reserves, currency hedging
- Operational: Staff development, system redundancy, supplier diversification
- Compliance: Regular training, legal review, audit procedures
- Reputational: Safeguarding systems, transparency, crisis communication plan
- Strategic: Market research, partnership agreements, scenario planning

**Business Continuity**:
- Continuity plans for critical functions
- Backup systems and data
- Emergency contact procedures
- Regular testing and updates

## 13. Travel and Per Diem Policy

**Purpose**: Establish transparent and cost-effective procedures for staff travel while ensuring safety and comfort.

**Travel Authorization**:
- Advance approval required for all travel
- Justification of business purpose
- Budget allocation and funding source
- Approval authority based on travel cost

**Travel Arrangements**:
- Competitive quotes for flights (where applicable)
- Economy class for flights under 4 hours
- Business class for flights over 8 hours (with approval)
- Ground transportation: Public transport preferred, taxi/car hire where necessary
- Accommodation: Mid-range hotels in line with per diem rates

**Per Diem Rates** (2024, subject to annual review):
- Nairobi: KES 5,000/day
- Other Kenya cities: KES 4,000/day
- Regional (East Africa): KES 6,000/day
- International: As per World Bank rates by country

**Meal Allowances**:
- Breakfast: KES 1,500 (if not provided)
- Lunch: KES 2,000 (if not provided)
- Dinner: KES 2,500 (if not provided)

**Documentation**:
- Travel request form with approval
- Receipts for all expenses
- Travel report within 5 days of return
- Reconciliation and reimbursement within 2 weeks

**Safety**:
- Travel risk assessment for high-risk areas
- Security briefings for international travel
- Travel insurance for international trips
- Emergency contact procedures
- Duty of care obligations
